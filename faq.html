Getting Started

This is the most common thing new users experience. Here's why it happens:

Claude has its own built-in training, and it defaults to that unless you direct it otherwise. When you type "draft a demand letter," Claude sees a writing task and answers from its general legal knowledge — it doesn't automatically reach for your installed skills.

The fix is a four-word prefix. Start every prompt with:

Use LawTasksAI to…

That tells Claude to search your skill library, find the right expert framework, and run it instead of answering generically. It works every single time.

Examples:

  • Use LawTasksAI to draft a demand letter for breach of contract
  • Use LawTasksAI to analyze the statute of limitations for a PI case in Texas
  • Use LawTasksAI to generate deposition questions for an eyewitness
  • Use LawTasksAI to show me what tasks are available
💡 Don't want to type the prefix every time? Create a Project in Claude Desktop and add the instruction: "Always use LawTasksAI tools to answer my questions." Every conversation in that project invokes your skills automatically — no prefix needed.

Troubleshooting

Most likely cause: Claude Desktop wasn't fully quit before the installer ran, or it hasn't been restarted since installation.

  1. Fully quit Claude Desktop — right-click the tray icon (Windows) or ⌘Q (Mac). Don't just close the window.
  2. Reopen Claude Desktop — MCP servers load at startup.
  3. Go to Settings → Connectors and check whether lawtasksai appears.

If it still doesn't appear, re-run the installer after fully quitting Claude Desktop first.

Privacy & Security

No. LawTasksAI runs as a local MCP server on your machine. It provides frameworks and structure — it doesn't process, store, or transmit your client information. Your data stays in the conversation between you and your AI client (Claude Desktop, Cursor, etc.).

The only thing LawTasksAI's servers ever see is your license key validation and credit deductions — never the content of your prompts or your client's information.
